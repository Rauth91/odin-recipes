Lasagna


This classic lasagna dish has been passed down through several generations. It is renowned for its layers of flat pasta, rich meat or vegetable sauce, creamy cheese, and gooey melted cheese on top. This hearty and comforting casserole is a favorite in many households and can be customized in various ways to suit different tastes.


Ingredients

For the Meat

  • 1 lb ground meat

For the Sauce

  • 28 oz diced tomatoes
  • 28 oz crushed tomatoes
  • 12 oz tomato paste
  • 8 oz tomato sauce
  • 2 ½ tablespoons oregano
  • 2 ½ tablespoons basil
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 tablespoons garlic (minced)
  • ½ tablespoon salt
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes

For Cheese Mixture

  • 1 large carton ricotta cheese
  • ½ cup Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • tablespoons parsley

For Assembly

  • 1 box no-boil lasagna noodles
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Additional Parmesan cheese for topping

Instrustion

  1. In a pan, cook the ground meat over medium heat until fully browned. - Drain and rinse the cooked meat, then set it aside for later use.
  2. In a large pot, combine the diced tomatoes, crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, and tomato sauce. - Season the sauce with oregano, basil, sugar, garlic, salt, and a pinch of red pepper flakes. - Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. In a mixing bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, Parmesan cheese, egg, and parsley. - Mix well and set aside.
  4. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). - Spread about 1 cup of the prepared sauce at the bottom of a 9x13x2 inch baking dish. - Place a layer of no-boil lasagna noodles over the sauce. - Pour half of the remaining sauce over the noodles, then sprinkle the cooked ground meat evenly over the sauce. - Add spoonfuls of the ricotta cheese mixture over the sauce and meat. - Sprinkle a layer of shredded mozzarella cheese over the ricotta mixture. - Place another layer of no-boil lasagna noodles on top. - Pour the remaining sauce over the noodles, ensuring they are fully covered. - Seal the dish tightly with aluminum foil.
  5. Bake the lasagna in the preheated oven for 1 hour. - Remove the foil, sprinkle additional Parmesan cheese on top, and bake uncovered for another 10-15 minutes or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  6. Allow the lasagna to set for about 10-15 minutes before serving to ensure it holds its shape. Enjoy your delicious homemade lasagna!
