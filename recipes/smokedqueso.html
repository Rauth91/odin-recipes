Smoked Queso

Bring a touch of family tradition to your table with this beloved Smoked Queso recipe, passed down through generations and perfected over time. This dish combines the creamy goodness of Velveeta and pepper jack cheese with the vibrant flavors of fresh jalapeños, green chili peppers, sweet onion, and red bell pepper. Browned ground beef adds a comforting heartiness, while a blend of cumin, smoked paprika, and chili powder gives it just the right amount of warmth and depth. Slowly smoked to perfection for two hours, this queso takes on a rich, smoky flavor that makes it truly special. Topped with a sprinkle of fresh cilantro and served with crispy tortilla chips, this queso is more than just a dip—it's a cherished family favorite that brings everyone together. Whether it's a holiday gathering, a weekend BBQ, or a cozy night in, this Smoked Queso is sure to create delicious memories.


Smoked Queso Recipe Guide

Ingredients Needed

Cheeses

  • 1 block Velveeta cheese, cubed
  • 1 block pepper jack cheese, cubed

Vegetables

  • 3 jalapeños, chopped (remove seeds for less heat)
  • 1 can whole green chili peppers, chopped
  • 2 cans chopped Rotel tomatoes (drained)
  • 1 sweet onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced

Meat

  • 1 lb. ground beef, browned and drained

Seasonings

  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder

Optional

  • 1/4 cup milk (for creaminess)

Garnish

  • Fresh cilantro, chopped

Serving

  • Tortilla chips
- Tortilla chips

How to Make Smoked Queso

Prepare the Ingredients

  1. Cube the Velveeta and pepper jack cheese.
  2. Brown the ground beef in a skillet over medium heat. Drain any excess fat.
  3. Dice the jalapeños (remove seeds for less heat).
  4. Chop the whole green chili peppers.
  5. Dice the sweet onion and red bell pepper.
  6. Mince the garlic.

Mix Ingredients

  1. In a large disposable aluminum pan, combine the cubed Velveeta, cubed pepper jack cheese, chopped jalapeños, chopped green chili peppers, drained Rotel tomatoes, diced sweet onion, diced red bell pepper, browned ground beef, and minced garlic.
  2. Add the cumin, smoked paprika, and chili powder. Mix well.
  3. If desired, add 1/4 cup of milk for a creamier texture.

Smoke the Queso

  1. Preheat your smoker to 200°F.
  2. Place the pan in the smoker and smoke for 2 hours, stirring every 30 minutes to ensure even melting and smoking.

Serve

  1. Once the cheese is fully melted and the flavors are well combined, remove the pan from the smoker.
  2. Garnish with fresh cilantro.
  3. Serve hot with tortilla chips.
